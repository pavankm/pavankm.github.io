About the Therapist

Ranjani (they/them) is a trauma informed and queer affirmative therapist. They are keenly aware of the social and cultural structures around us, and thus can hold a holistic perspective towards mental wellbeing.

Ranj believes that people have an innate ability towards growth and authenticity. They help their clients have rewarding and fulfilling relationships with themselves and others by exploring together their histories and understanding patterns. In therapy, they create a non-directive and reflective space where the client is the expert of their own narrative. Ranj is a certified queer affirmative therapist, trained by Mariwala Health Initiative, and graduated from NIMHANS with their MPhil degree.

They are working and continuing to train and grow as a therapist for the past 4 years. They speak English, Tamil and Hindi.

You might find Ranj chattering away about anime and books sometimes, and the best way to pleasantly distract Ranj is to let your furry baby wander into the session.

About Musubi

In Japanese, musubi is connection and flow. It is understood as the power of becoming and creation. The word is now part of this process as a reminder of the client’s agency to connect with themselves, weaving the narrative with curiosity, compassion and insight.
